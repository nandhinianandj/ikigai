You will:

  • Lead from the power of your own story
  • Connect with people and strengthen them from within
  • Discover how to transform limitations to possibilities

A dragonfly got one of the wings wounded while escaping a trap. Another dragonfly, a friend with empowering leadership qualities, saw them in that painful state. Pick the friend’s response.

At least one option is required!
It can be good at times to see problems as small so we can feel powerful enough to overcome them. It is even more powerful to build resilience and support. "I cannot imagine your pain, We have been through tough things before, and I am here to support you as you recover." is a more empowering response
Recognizing and respecting their own view of their experience shows you have empathy. Building on past shared success and togetherness helps the injured dragonfly feel
Emphasizing and encouraging a "rise to the challenge" can be helpful by turning the focus from the hurt to the recovery. It is even more empowering to emphasize the dragonfly’s capability of resilience and support. The response "I cannot imagine your pain. We have been through tough things before, and I am here to support you as you recover." empowers the dragonfly by focusing on their strength rather than the size of the challenge.

A pack of ducklings was following their stern parents as they learned how to swim efficiently against high tides. The parent was a skillful leader. Pick the parent’s empowering approach to teaching.

At least one option is required!
I can be helpful to lead and show best practices. Additionally, it is empowering to focus on what is being done right, so the learners build on their own best practices while learning from others. The response “The parent assisted each duckling to follow their natural way of swimming and how they can build on that while learning new ways.” is more empowering.
Rewards can motivate people but often do little for people struggling to learn. It can be more empowering to focus on the learning in addition to the achievement. The response “The parent assisted each duckling to follow their natural way of swimming and how they can build on that while learning new ways.” is more empowering to the group.
This approach builds on the individual strengths of each learner as they increase their capability.

A young panda only loved to eat the new and fresh bamboo shoots. It would often climb the tallest bamboo tree where the new growth was plentiful. One day, being too engrossed in devouring the favorite snack, the panda fell hard on the ground and was seriously injured. It took some time to heal and even longer for the panda to be comfortable climbing bamboo trees again. What response to the fall did the panda use to overcome the fear.

At least one option is required!
I can be helpful to lead and show best practices. Additionally, it is empowering to focus on what is being done right, so the learners build on their own best practices while learning from others. The response “The parent assisted each duckling to follow their natural way of swimming and how they can build on that while learning new ways.” is more empowering.
Trying to forget an important event can make it less likely that the event is actually forgotten and that any lessons are learned. It is more empowering to focus the memory on the empowering aspects of the event. Remembering the mistake that led to the fall along with all the things done successfully is more empowering.
It is very empowering to focus on what you learn and what you value than it is to focus on the pain or loss. This response helped the panda recover and enjoy eating bamboo again.
